About Lei

Yanglei Zheng,

Welcome to a journey through the life and artistry of a dedicated watercolor painter. This portfolio showcases a lifetime of creative exploration, where every stroke captures the beauty of nature, emotion, and imagination. With a deep passion for storytelling through vibrant hues and delicate washes, these works reflect years of growth, dedication, and achievements in the world of watercolor painting. Each piece tells a story, celebrating the milestones and moments that define a life devoted to art.

Lei' s artwork has been exhibited in prominent venues, including Van Dusen Gardens in 2023, UBC Alumni Centre in 2022, and Art Works Gallery in 2021, showcasing her talent in watercolor painting.

She has received prestigious awards such as the Canadian Watercolor Society Gold Medal in 2022 and the UBC Art Excellence Award in 2020, recognizing her artistic innovation and skill.

Her works have been featured in renowned publications like The Art of Watercolor magazine, West Coast Artists Quarterly, and UBC's Creativity Journal, gaining her widespread recognition.

Lei has shared her expertise through workshops and masterclasses, including “The Art of Water & Light” and guest lectures at UBC Fine Arts Club, inspiring aspiring artists.

Experience

Lei began her journey as a watercolor artist during her time at the University of British Columbia, where she discovered her passion for expressing emotions and stories through delicate brushstrokes. Initially drawn to painting as a form of relaxation, she soon realized that watercolor provided the perfect medium to capture the fleeting beauty of nature and the subtle interplay of light and shadow.

Over the years, Lei has honed her craft by experimenting with various techniques, from wet-on-wet blending to fine detailing. Her work is heavily inspired by the landscapes of British Columbia, with its serene coastlines, lush forests, and ever-changing skies. She often incorporates a minimalist aesthetic, allowing her art to evoke tranquility and invite viewers to interpret the emotions behind each piece.

In addition to creating her own collections, Lei has participated in several group and solo exhibitions, sharing her work with diverse audiences. She has also taught watercolor workshops, guiding students to explore their own creativity and find their unique style. Lay believes that art is not just about technical skill but also about connecting with others and sharing meaningful stories.
